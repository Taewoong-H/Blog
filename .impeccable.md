## Design Context

### Users
Readers of Taewoong.dev are people who want to read concise personal records about frontend development, daily routines, travel, and money. They visit to scan recent writing, browse by topic, and read individual MDX posts comfortably.

### Brand Personality
The blog should feel personal, crisp, and editorial. It should present a working developer's notes with enough polish to feel intentional, without becoming a marketing site.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the provided Taewoong.dev design as the visual source of truth: warm off-white background, white article surfaces, sharp blue accent, compact sticky header, category-first archive sections, mono metadata, and restrained placeholder cover blocks. The interface is light-mode first and should avoid dark neon, oversized hero marketing, and generic card-heavy decoration.

### Design Principles
- Make categories visible: development, life, travel, and economy are first-class navigation and content sections.
- Keep writing central: visual structure should improve scanning without distracting from post titles and MDX content.
- Use precise details: tight metadata, clean borders, subtle hover states, and restrained radius values.
- Preserve maintainability: implement the design with reusable React components and CSS classes rather than importing the DC runtime.
